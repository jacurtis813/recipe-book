Bacon and Egg Burrito

  • Time: 25 Minutes
  • Serves: 4
Ingredients Quantity
Bacon Strips Diced 6 Slices
Hash Potatoes Cubed 1 Cup
Eggs (Brown or White) 6 Eggs
Sour Cream 1/4 Cup
Shredded Cheddar Cheese 3/4 Cup
Hot Pepper Sauce 1 Teaspoon
Flour Tortillas (10in) 4 Tortillas

Step 1: In a large skillet, cook bacon over medium heat until crisp. Using a slotted spoon, remove to paper towels; drain, reserving 1 tablespoon drippings.

Step 2: Saute potatoes and onions in drippings until potatoes are golden brown, stirring occasionally.

Step 3: In a large bowl, whisk eggs and sour cream. Stir in 1/4 cup cheese, taco sauce and hot pepper sauce.

Step 4: Pour over potato mixture; add bacon. Cook and stir over medium heat until eggs are completely set.

Step 5: Spoon about 3/4 cup down the center of each tortilla; sprinkle with remaining cheese. Fold bottom and sides of tortilla over filling.

Step 6: Serve immediately with sour cream and tomatoes if desired.

White Chicken Chili

  • Time: 40-45 Minutes
  • Servings: 10 1/2
Ingedients Quantity
Boneless Skinless Chicken Breast 1 Pound
Olive Oil 1 Tablespoon
Garlic Cloves Minced 2 Cloves
Chicken Broth 2 (14 oz.) Cans
Chopped Green Chiles 1 (4 oz.) Can
Ground Cumin 2 Teaspoons
Dried Oregano 2 Teaspoons
Cayenne Pepper 1 Teaspoons
Great Nothern Beans Drained 3 (14 oz.) Cans
Shredded Monterey Jack Cheese 1 Cup

Step 1: In a Dutch oven over medium heat, cook chicken and onion in oil until lightly browned.

Step 2: Add garlic; cook 1 minute longer. Stir in the broth, chiles, cumin, oregano and cayenne; bring to a boil.

Step 3: Reduce heat to low. With a potato masher, mash one can of beans until smooth. Add to saucepan

Step 4: Add remaining beans to saucepan. Simmer for 20-30 minutes or until chicken is no longer pink and onion is tender.

Step 5: Top each serving with cheese and, if desired, jalapeno pepper.

No Bake Cookies

  • Time: 20-25 Minutes
  • Servings: 2 1/2 Dozen
Ingedients Quantity
Granulated Sugar 2 Cups
Butter Cubed 1/2 Cup
2% Milk 1/2 Cup
Baking Cocoa 3 Tablespoons
Creamy Peanut Butter 1/2 Cup
Vanilla Extract 1 Teaspoon
OLd Fashioned Oats 3 Cups
Salt Just a Pinch

Step 1 : In a large saucepan, combine the first 5 ingredients.

Step 2 : Bring to a boil, stirring constantly. Cook and stir 3 minutes.

Step 3 : Remove from heat; stir in peanut butter and vanilla until blended.

Step 4: Stir in the old fashioned oats in the mixture. Stir thoroughly.

Step 5: Drop mixture by tablespoonfuls onto waxed paper-lined baking sheets. Refrigerate until set. Store in airtight containers.
